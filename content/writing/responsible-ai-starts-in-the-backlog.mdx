## Overview

Responsible AI is not a policy document. It is a set of delivery decisions that happen every week. The backlog is where those decisions start, because it is where you choose what you will build, how you will measure it, and what you will accept as risk.

This article outlines a practical approach that keeps ethics in the delivery loop without slowing teams down.

## The moment ethics becomes delivery work

Ethical issues appear long before a model ships. They show up when a team decides:

- which data to collect
- who will be affected by the outcome
- what the system should do when it is wrong

If those decisions are left to the end, teams will be forced into defensive fixes. If they are considered at the start, they become normal engineering trade offs.

## A working definition of responsible AI

For most product teams, responsible AI means:

- the system behaves predictably for users
- the failure modes are understood and visible
- the impact is clear to the people affected
- there is a human escape route when the system gets it wrong

This definition is practical and testable. It keeps the focus on user impact rather than vague promises.

## Where it fits in the backlog

The backlog is the best place to make ethics real because:

- it is where scope is set
- it is where acceptance criteria live
- it is where engineering teams negotiate risk

If you can express ethical concerns as acceptance criteria, teams can build them with confidence.

## A delivery checklist that works

Use a simple checklist at planning time:

1. **Data and consent**  
   What data is used, and can users opt out?

2. **Impact group**  
   Who benefits and who could be harmed?

3. **Failure mode**  
   What happens when the system is wrong?

4. **Visibility**  
   Can users and operators understand the outcome?

5. **Override**  
   Is there a human route to correction?

6. **Auditability**  
   Can decisions be reviewed later?

## Practical example

Imagine a system that matches suppliers to a project. An ethical review might add these delivery notes:

- Show the factors behind the match.
- Log the decision data for review.
- Allow manual override for edge cases.

None of these slow the work, but all of them make the system more accountable.

## Closing thought

Responsible AI is a delivery habit. Put it in the backlog and it becomes part of the build. Leave it to the end and it becomes a crisis.

If you are building AI systems, start there.
