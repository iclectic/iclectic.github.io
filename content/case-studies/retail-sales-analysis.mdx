## Overview

This project involved building a comprehensive SQL-based analysis pipeline for retail sales data. The goal was to extract actionable business insights from raw transactional data using structured querying techniques.

## The Problem

Retail businesses generate vast amounts of transactional data daily, but without structured analysis, this data remains underutilised. The challenge was to design a schema, clean the data, and run targeted analyses to surface patterns in sales performance, customer behaviour, and product trends.

## Approach

I started by designing a normalised database schema in PostgreSQL, then wrote a series of SQL queries that progressed from basic exploration to complex analytical operations.

### Schema Design

The schema was designed to capture transactions with fields for date, product category, quantity, unit price, total amount, and customer demographics. I used appropriate data types and constraints to ensure data integrity from the start.

### Data Cleaning

Before analysis, I performed several cleaning steps:

- Removed duplicate transaction records
- Handled null values in customer demographic fields
- Validated date ranges and price consistency
- Standardised category names

### Analysis

The analysis covered several business questions:

1. **Total sales by month** to identify seasonal patterns
2. **Best-selling product categories** by both quantity and revenue
3. **Customer segmentation** based on purchase frequency and spend
4. **Time-of-day analysis** to understand peak shopping hours
5. **Year-over-year growth** calculations

## Key Findings

The analysis revealed clear seasonal patterns in purchasing behaviour, with Q4 consistently outperforming other quarters. Certain product categories showed strong correlation with customer demographics, suggesting targeted marketing opportunities.

## Technical Details

- **Database**: PostgreSQL 15
- **Query complexity**: CTEs, window functions, subqueries, aggregations
- **Data volume**: 10,000+ transaction records
- **Tools**: pgAdmin, DBeaver for query development

## What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of clean data as the foundation for any analysis. It also deepened my understanding of PostgreSQL window functions and their power for time-series analysis in retail contexts.
